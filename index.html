About This Playlist

As listeners navigate through the selected works of the playlist, they’ll notice shifts in the theme of “Love and Relationships,” specifically from the perspective of more traditional, white artists to more contemporary, African-American ones. For example, William Shakespeare’s “Sonnet 116” emphasizes the stability and consistency in romantic relationships, presenting a very positive view of love. Alternatively, Langston Huges’s “Love Song for Lucinda” is much more skeptical, asserting that love holds many dangers hidden behind its surface level appeals. The difference in perspectives between the white and black artists in this playlist can be attributed to the societal prejudice that the African-American artists had to endure due to racism and sexism. These experiences very well may have caused these artists to form views of human relationships that are much more critical and cautious than those of their white, older counterparts.
